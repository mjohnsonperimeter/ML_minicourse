Understanding and Effectively Using AI and ML Tools in Physics and Astronomy 

The rapidly evolving landscape of artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) tools, from ChatGPT to bespoke algorithms for large experimental datasets, is changing the way we perform both theoretical and experimental research in physics. In this minicourse, we will give an overview of these tools with examples of how they can be effectively and reliably used. We will also discuss what lies under the hood of these tools, covering the basics of neural networks and training dynamics, deep learning, scaling laws, and interpretability. The course will be structured around lectures and group exercises.
